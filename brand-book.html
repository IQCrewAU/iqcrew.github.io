02 — The mark & the stamp

One quiet mark. One loud stamp.

The mark

iq
IQ Crew
  • Lowercase monogram in a rounded square (radius = 25% of side). Eucalypt Deep on Paper, or Paper on Eucalypt Deep. Mono (single-colour) versions in Ink, plus a Paper reverse for dark surfaces; in both, the letterforms knock out to transparent.
  • Clearspace: the height of the "i" on all sides. Minimum size: 24 px monogram, 90 px full lockup. Ready-made files for every context ship in Asset Kit v1 (logos, social, email, favicon, stamp).
  • Never gradients, shadows, outlines, or recolouring. Never on photography. Never animated.

The stamp — adopted from Direction 02

A verification device, not a decoration. It certifies that the artefact it sits on is evidenced.
  • Where it may appear: Scorecard reports, assessment deliverables, published case studies, the Trust Centre, and evidence packs. Nowhere else.
  • Rotation −6° to −12°, never 0°. A straight stamp is a badge; a tilted stamp was pressed by a hand.

  • Never on the homepage hero, ads, or anything unverified. A stamp on marketing fluff kills the device permanently.

06 — Signature devices

Three things only we do

1 · The boundary motif

All illustration derives from the real architecture: a person outside, a bordered dedicated environment inside, a governed path between. Drawn in Eucalypt line-work on Paper or Tint. Labels stay country-generic ("your offshore team"). No other illustration style exists — no 3D renders, no flat-people scenes, no icons-as-decoration.

2 · The receipt

The voice slider "technical receipts one click deep" made literal. Any claim on the site can open into its mechanism. This is the site's defining interaction — try it:

Every client runs in their own dedicated Microsoft Azure tenant.RECEIPT
MECHANISM  — tenant provisioned per client at onboarding; no shared subscriptions
ENFORCED   — isolation boundary set at deployment; cannot be disabled per-user
VERIFIED   — automated audit, monthly · evidence pack available under NDA
REGISTER   — claim A2 · status PRINT NOW
Client environments are deployed exclusively to Australian Azure regions.RECEIPT
MECHANISM  — region allow-list: Australian regions only
ENFORCED   — Azure Policy at deployment; non-Australian regions are denied, not discouraged
VERIFIED   — continuous policy compliance scan
SCOPE      — platform metadata handled per Microsoft; see Trust Centre scope note · claim A3
  • Receipt content rules: mechanism, enforcement point, verification cadence, register row. In mono, under 5 lines, sentence fragments.
  • Receipts never expose internals — no script names, file paths, repo names, vendor product names, or infrastructure identifiers. Disclosure discipline is part of the security posture.
  • A claim that can't produce a receipt doesn't go on the site. The device polices the copy.

08 — Design doctrine

Built by hand: how this site refuses to look generated

Sites read as AI-made because they're assembled from defaults. The counter isn't more decoration — it's fewer defaults, real content, and one idea executed with unusual discipline. These rules are hard constraints for the build; the CLAUDE.md will carry them verbatim.

Named tells — banned outright

  • The three-feature-card row with rounded icon chips. Features are told as prose sections with boundary-motif diagrams and receipts.
  • Centred hero over a gradient blob or mesh. Our hero is left-set on the asymmetric grid with the data-path drawing.
  • Terracotta/warm-clay accents (the #D97757 family) — the single most recognisable AI-design colour. Nothing in our palette approaches it; Record Red is quarantined to the stamp.
  • Glassmorphism, purple gradients, floating 3D shapes, dark-mode-with-acid-green.
  • Fake proof: logo walls, "trusted by 10,000+", invented testimonials, counters animating to made-up numbers. Our proof is CFC, receipts, and published pricing.
  • Scroll-triggered fade-up on every section. Testimonial carousels. Emoji. Generic icon sets as decoration.
  • Card-grid-with-shadow as the default layout unit. Every section earns a bespoke structure.
  • Copy that never commits — no prices, no names, no numbers. We publish the price, name the client, state the region.

The replacement grammar

  • Asymmetric by default: 7/5 and 8/4 column splits; text blocks hang off a consistent left axis; whitespace is a material, not a gap.
  • Receipts as rhythm: the claim→receipt device recurs down the page, so the site's texture is itself the proof argument.
  • Honest tables: pricing is a real table with real numbers, mono figures, and the minimum stated plainly. Tables are a feature of the voice, not a failure of imagination.
  • One orchestrated moment: on load, the hero data-path draws itself once (~900 ms, SVG stroke). Receipts expand. Hover states shift ≤150 ms. Nothing else moves. prefers-reduced-motion disables the draw.
  • Real specificity everywhere: australiaeast not "the cloud"; "one business day" not "24/7 world-class support"; "3 of 11 accounts" not "several issues".
  • The idiosyncrasy budget is spent: the tilted stamp and the quarantined red are the system's two deliberate oddities. Nothing else gets to be weird.
